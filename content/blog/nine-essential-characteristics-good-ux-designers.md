<p>Fred Beecher wrote what he thinks are the nine essential characteristics you must possess to make a good software user experience designer, in reaction to a comment made about skills an IA should possess. It's an excellent read for those listing qualifications and evaluating UX Designers for hire, and a great list of qualities for job-seekers to demonstrate during interviews.</p>
<ol>
<li>A Deep Understanding of Human Psychology &amp; Research Methods</li>
<li>Competence in the Basics of Graphic Design</li>
<li>An Awareness of and Interest in Technology</li>
<li>Verbal &amp; Visual Communication Skills</li>
<li>Moderate Familiarity with Business, Deep Familiarity with Your Business</li>
<li>The Ability to Quickly Learn a Subject Matter Area</li>
<li>Mediation, Facilitation, &amp; Translation Skills</li>
<li>Creativity &amp; Vision</li>
<li>Passion</li>
</ol>
<p>Elizabeth Buie also added a familiarity with HCI literature, and undboubtedly with HCI history, which I think should be there as well.
